About the Collection

In a world consumed by digital screens, nature has become a distant memory—a casualty of relentless global warming, pollution, and unchecked industrialisation. Forests have withered, oceans suffocate under layers of waste, and skies are shrouded in a perpetual haze. With the physical world fading away, people have retreated into their digital realms, numbing themselves to the loss outside. Yet, there’s a faint, almost painful longing for the natural beauty that once was. This virtual window offers a glimpse—a manufactured, imperfect recollection of the world we destroyed. It’s an eerie approximation, pieced together from memories blurred by time and distance, a reminder of the paradise we once had but have now forgotten.
